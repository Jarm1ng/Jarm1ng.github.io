About

Researcher profile

I am a researcher in medical image analysis and surgical computer vision, currently pursuing an MSc in Advanced Computer Science at the University of Leeds, where I collaborate with the AI in Medicine and Surgery Group under Dr Sharib Ali.

My research centres on computer vision for image-guided liver intervention — connecting preoperative 3D anatomy to the intraoperative laparoscopic view through geometry-aware 3D-2D registration, anatomical landmark segmentation, and augmented-reality guidance for surgery.

I am broadly interested in building clinically grounded systems that bring reliable 3D understanding into the operating room.

Vis2Reg: Visibility-Aware Landmark-Free Geometric 3D-2D Registration for Liver Laparoscopy

Jiaming Feng, X. Zhang, S. Farid, S. Ali

Medical Image Computing and Computer Assisted Intervention (MICCAI), 2026

A visibility-aware, landmark-free geometric 3D-2D registration framework for laparoscopic liver surgery, using differentiable rasterization and mask-guided self-supervision for robust alignment under severe partial visibility.

Jiaming Feng, X. Zhang, S. Farid, S. Ali

A geometry-guided multi-branch mesh Transformer for 3D liver surface landmark segmentation — hierarchical mesh encoding, directional feature fusion, and multi-task supervision; 59.5% overall Dice and 4.4 mm Chamfer Distance on a liver mesh benchmark.
